    title: "Strategy bought 3,273 BTC for $255 million on April 27 — its second purchase in eight days, pushing total holdings to 818,334 BTC."
    summary: >-
      On April 27, Strategy disclosed the acquisition of 3,273 Bitcoin at an average price of
      approximately $77,900 per coin, totaling $255 million, funded through the sale of roughly
      1.45 million shares of Class A common stock via the firm's $21 billion at-the-market equity
      offering program. The purchase followed the record 34,164-BTC acquisition disclosed April 20
      and brings Strategy's cumulative holdings to 818,334 BTC — acquired for a total cost basis of
      approximately $61.81 billion at an average of $75,537 per coin. The company now controls
      approximately 3.9% of the 21 million coin hard cap. Executive Chairman Michael Saylor has
      stated a goal of reaching one million BTC; at the current pace, Strategy sits 181,666 coins
      short of that target.
    consequence: >-
      The funding mechanism is notable: the April 27 purchase drew on the MSTR common stock ATM
      rather than the STRC preferred stock program used for the prior week's $2.54 billion
      acquisition. Strategy is deliberately cycling across multiple instruments — equity ATM, STRC
      preferred, STRK preferred — to sustain accumulation without concentration risk in any single
      capital source. The firm's total authorized fundraising capacity across all three programs
      stands at approximately $44 billion.

    title: "Senators Tillis and Alsobrooks struck a stablecoin yield deal on May 1 — clearing the Clarity Act's last obstacle and scheduling a Senate Banking markup for the week of May 11."
    summary: >-
      After months of impasse, Senators Thom Tillis and Angela Alsobrooks released compromise
      language on May 1 resolving the stablecoin yield dispute that had stalled the Clarity Act
      since its missed April markup deadline. The deal bans yield equivalent to bank deposit
      interest — the provision the banking lobby required — while explicitly permitting
      activity-based rewards tied to actual platform usage, the floor the crypto industry
      demanded. By May 2, Coinbase, Circle, and the Crypto Council for Innovation had all
      publicly endorsed the compromise, and the Senate Banking Committee announced a markup
      target for the week of May 11 — the last available slot before the Memorial Day recess
      closes the chamber on May 21. For Bitcoin specifically, the bill's defining provision
      classifies BTC as a CFTC-regulated digital commodity, providing the permanent legislative
      certainty that has kept some institutional custodians cautious about offering direct
      Bitcoin exposure. Four steps remain between the markup and a presidential signature: a
      60-vote Senate floor threshold, reconciliation between Senate Agriculture and Banking
      Committee versions, reconciliation with the House-passed text from July 2025, and the
      president's signature.
    consequence: >-
      Polymarket odds for the Clarity Act being signed into law in 2026 moved to 68% following
      the deal announcement. The week of May 11 is the last realistic markup window before the
      legislative calendar tightens ahead of November's midterms — a missed slot would push the
      next viable opportunity to September or later.
