    title: "A quantum-safe Bitcoin transaction scheme published this week requires no protocol changes — and no miner consensus."
    summary: >-
      Named QSB, the scheme was published April 9 by StarkWare CPO Avihu Levy and works entirely within
      Bitcoin's existing script rules — no soft fork, no miner signaling required. It relies on
      RIPEMD-160 pre-image resistance, a hash function quantum computers can only attack via Grover's
      algorithm, which yields a quadratic rather than exponential speedup, leaving roughly 118 bits of
      effective security. Each transaction costs $75–$150 in GPU compute and routes directly to miners
      outside the normal mempool. The tradeoffs are significant: protection applies only to newly created
      outputs, Lightning Network compatibility is absent, and the estimated 4 million BTC in legacy P2PK
      addresses — the most quantum-vulnerable format — remains unaffected.
    consequence: >-
      Levy frames QSB explicitly as a last-resort contingency, not a replacement for a protocol-level
      upgrade — but it establishes that a quantum emergency response exists within Bitcoin's current
      ruleset, requiring no one's permission to deploy.
